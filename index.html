Calvary, c. 1470/1480

"Calvary" (c. 1470/1480), attributed to the Master of the Fiesole Epiphany, depicts the Crucifixion of Christ, with figures of the Virgin Mary, Saint John the Evangelist, and Mary Magdalene mourning at the foot of the cross. The painting features a dramatic landscape and expressive figures, highlighting the emotional intensity of the scene. Its detailed composition and use of color reflect the style of late 15th-century Italian religious art.
Artists / Makers: Master of the Death of Saint Nicholas of Münster (painter) German, active c. 1460 - 1490

The Annunciation with Saint Francis and Saint Louis of Toulouse [four panels], c. 1470/1480

"The Annunciation with Saint Francis and Saint Louis of Toulouse" (c. 1470/1480) by the Master of the Fiesole Epiphany is a set of four panels depicting the Annunciation scene, where the angel Gabriel announces to the Virgin Mary that she will conceive Jesus. The side panels feature Saint Francis of Assisi and Saint Louis of Toulouse, symbolizing humility and devotion. The work is characterized by its elegant figures, rich colors, and a harmonious composition typical of late 15th-century Italian art.
Artists / Makers: Cosmè Tura (painter) Ferrarese, c. 1433 - 1495

The Feast of Herod and the Beheading of Saint John the Baptist, 1461-1462

"The Feast of Herod and the Beheading of Saint John the Baptist" (1461–1462) by Benozzo Gozzoli depicts the biblical story of Saint John the Baptist's beheading at the request of Salome during a feast held by King Herod. The painting captures three sequential scenes within a single composition: Salome's dance, the execution of John the Baptist, and the presentation of his head to Herod. Gozzoli's work is noted for its detailed figures, vivid colors, and elaborate architectural background.
Artists / Makers: Benozzo Gozzoli (artist) Florentine, c. 1421 - 1497
